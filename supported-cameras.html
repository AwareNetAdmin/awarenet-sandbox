Supported Cameras

Below is the current list of cameras that do meet the requirements to work with AwareNet. We do our best to keep this information accurate but it is not guaranteed. Other camera brands/models will be added to the list as soon as we test them. To request support for a camera not listed below, tell us the make and model of the camera.

The minimum requirements for a camera are:

  • The ability to identify motion as a person, vehicle, or something else.
  • The ability to send an email notification or webhook to AwareNet (when a motion recording is captured). The email/webhook must include (1) the camera name (2) type of motion (person, vehicle, other), and (3) the video clip.

Axis

Available at www.axis.com. All models using Axis OS 11 or above should be compatible with AwareNet.
Typical price is $600 - $5,000 per camera.
Feel free to contact us with your questions.

Reolink

Available at www.reolink.com and www.amazon.com. All current models for sale by Reolink should be compatible with AwareNet.
Typical price is $100 - $200 per camera.

If you have a standard outlet available to power a camera, the Reolink Duo 3 WiFi is most often the best option. It is suitable for locating on a house or garage up to 35 feet from the street or alley. You will also need a 256GB class 10 data card. The Elite Floodlight WiFi is a good option if you need/want to really light up a dark area.

If you can do Power over Ethernet (PoE), the Reolink Duo 3 PoE is an excellent option.

If you must have a battery-powered camera, the Argus 4 Pro camera with matching solar panel is a good option. It is suitable for locating adjacent to the street or alley. It is not a good choice for installing at a location that is more than 10 feet from the sidewalk/street or areas with continuous people/vehicle traffic. You will also need a 256GB class 10 data card.

Many other models are available for specific situations. Feel free to contact us with your questions.

Unsupported Cameras

Below is the current list of commonly available cameras that do not meet the requirements to work with AwareNet. We do our best to keep this information accurate but it is not guaranteed.

Amcrest

The Amcrest cameras with AI person & vehicle detection have the ability to send email alerts (but not webhook alerts). However, there is no option to include the video clip with the email. So the cameras do not meet the minimum requirements to work with AwareNet.

Blink

Unfortunately, Blink cameras do not meet the minimum requirements to work with AwareNet because they cannot send an email or webhook to AwareNet when the camera senses motion.

Nest

The Nest Outdoor camera can send an email (but not a webhook) when the camera senses motion. However, the only option is to send the email to the email account the camera is registered to. Depending on what email account the camera is registered to, it might be possible to configure that account to automatically forward the alerts to AwareNet. If you have a Nest Outdoor camera and would like to try this, please contact us.

Ring

Unfortunately, Ring cameras do not meet the minimum requirements to work with AwareNet because they cannot send an email or webhook to AwareNet when the camera senses motion.

SimpliSafe

The SimpliSafe camera with person detection has the ability to send email alerts. However, there is no option to include the video clip with the email. And it cannot send a webhook. So the camera does not meet the minimum requirements to work with AwareNet.

Wyze

Unfortunately, Wyze cameras do not meet the minimum requirements to work with AwareNet because they cannot send an email or webhook to AwareNet when the camera senses motion.
