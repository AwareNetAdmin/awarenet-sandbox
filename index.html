Deploy Security Cameras

A traditional neighborhood watch puts residents outside at night, risking their own safety, to observe activity. A traditional security patrol is limited to line-of-sight, often missing important activity.

Security cameras change the equation. Mounted on houses, garages, and business buildings, they monitor streets, alleys, and parking lots - all at the same time, with no risk to volunteers.

Each participating camera is configured once to automatically send alerts at night (typically 10pm - 6am), including a short video clip, to AwareNet whenever person or vehicle motion is detected by the camera. After configuration, the camera operates every night, without any action by the camera owner.

Monitor Automated Alerts

With a neighborhood network of security cameras established, the next key step is to monitor the automated alerts arriving at AwareNet.

How It Works

  • Motion Alerts: Neighborhood cameras automatically detect person and vehicle motion during monitored hours (10pm-6am) and send real-time alerts, including a video clip, to AwareNet
  • Real-Time Reviews: Your designated security professionals, or trusted volunteers, use the AwareNet web app on a tablet or laptop to review incoming alerts in real-time to identify suspicious or criminal activity
  • Progress Tracking — Reviewers categorize alerts as they go, so it's always clear what has been seen and what still needs attention

Activate Professional Response

Using real-time detection to trigger real-time action elevates your neighborhood to the gold standard for protection.

An on-location security officer responding directly or a volunteer reviewer calling 911 — either way, a professional response to suspicious and criminal activity is transformative. Your neighborhood is no longer an easy target for criminals.

The long-term strategy is to create a lasting crime deterrence through consistent, professional response.
