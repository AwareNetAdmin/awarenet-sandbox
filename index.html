Alert Monitoring

Reviewing alerts is the foundation of our crime disruption system.

How It Works

  • Motion Alerts: Neighborhood cameras automatically detect person and vehicle movement during monitored hours (10pm-6am) and send real-time alerts, including a video clip, to AwareNet.
  • Real-Time Reviews: Your designated security professional, or trusted volunteer, use the AwareNet web app to review incoming alerts in real-time to identify any suspicious or criminal activity.
  • Record Keeping: Alerts are categorized by the reviewer as they go to easily track which have been reviewed

Key Benefits

  • 24/7 vigilance during peak crime hours
  • No false positive fatigue ‚Äî immediate human review by your designated reviewer
  • Works with your existing camera investment
  • Comprehensive neighborhood coverage

This critical first phase ensures that no suspicious activity goes unnoticed, forming the eyes and ears of your neighborhood's defense system.

Professional Response

Professional response is where detection transforms into action. Your designated reviewer ‚Äî a volunteer or hired security officer ‚Äî doesn't just watch; they actively intervene to disrupt criminal activity as it unfolds.

Immediate Intervention

  • Real-time Decision Making: Within seconds of alert review, your reviewer determines the appropriate response level
  • Direct Communication: Contact with authorities, property owners, or security services as needed
  • Coordinated Response: Multiple response options including police dispatch, security patrol, or resident notification
  • Documentation: Complete incident logging for follow-up and pattern analysis

Response Options

  • Emergency services dispatch for active crimes
  • Property owner/resident immediate notification
  • Security patrol deployment to area
  • Coordinated neighbor watch activation

  • Pattern Recognition: Criminals quickly learn which neighborhoods actively respond to incidents
  • Word-of-Mouth Effect: Consistent intervention creates reputation among criminal networks
  • Behavioral Change: Criminals choose easier targets, avoiding neighborhoods with active response
  • Community Confidence: Residents feel safer knowing their neighborhood actively fights crime

The Compounding Effect

  • Each successful intervention reinforces the deterrent effect
  • Criminals share information about "hard targets" to avoid
  • Property values benefit from reduced crime reputation
  • Community cohesion strengthens through shared security
