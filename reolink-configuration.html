Reolink Camera Configuration

The screenshot below shows a typical main Settings page from the Reolink iOS app for a Reolink camera. Your menu may be different depending on your camera and other factors.

At the top of the Settings page, next to a picture of the camera, it shows:

  • The name you have given the camera (e.g. 123 Main Street)
  • The model of the camera (e.g. Reolink Duo 2 WiFi)
  • The amount used and capacity of the data card in the camera

Click on the camera name to display the Device Info page

Information for each of the other options is listed below. In some cases, a link is provided to see more details about the option. When appropriate, an option is identified as CRITICAL or Important. Please give these extra attention.

Network

Selecting your WiFi Network. Once this is set during the initial camera setup, you probably don't need to do anything here. This does not apply for PoE cameras.

Display

Important. All things related to what you see on live and recorded video.

Device Volume

Optional. Control the volume for the speaker (used for you talking to someone and for the alert siren).

Detection/Alarm

Important. Control the Detection Zone, Sensitivity, and Object Size.

Camera Recording

CRITICAL. Control what is recorded to the data card in the camera (or, optionally, to the cloud).

Push Notifications

Optional. It controls which events/alerts appear on your smartphone. There are separate settings (see Email Alerts below) for which events/alerts are sent to AwareNet. Many camera owners choose to leave this turned off.

Email Alerts

CRITICAL! This option controls which events/alerts are sent to AwareNet. It is CRITICAL to the success of AwareNet to set this correctly. There are separate settings for which events/alerts are displayed on your smartphone.

FTP Upload

You can ignore this. It is used only by "techies" that want to store their video clips on an separate storage server.

Siren

This feature is not typically used. However, it can be useful as an additional deterrent, but may annoy nearby neighbors. Instead of triggering a default siren sound from the camera according to a schedule, it can play a 5 second recording / announcement (e.g. "Alert, your activity is subject to real-time monitoring and response").

Share Camera

Optional. Displays the UID (unique camera id) and a QR code which is a digital representation of the UID. To give someone access to your camera, you must give them either the UID or the QR code, plus a userid and a password.

Advanced

Optional. Manage userids & passwords, set the date/time, control sound recording, the infrared lights, and the spotlight. Although not required, it is often helpful to have audio recorded with the video clips so please turn on Record Audio. The other settings here can be left as-is.
